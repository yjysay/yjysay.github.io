The first time I've seen a film in IMAX was back in 2009 when I watched Star Trek. While I wasn't a huge fan of the film at the time because it was the first time I saw anything related to Star Trek and my expectation was different, I still didn't feel like I wasted my money for nothing. After all, for what it's worth, the film looked pretty neat on IMAX screen. While I continued to watch flims in IMAX, it wasn't until 2013 when I watched Pacific Rim in IMAX and was absolutely blown away by it. While one could debate the actual quality of the film, I still found it to be one of the most visually outstanding films I've ever seen (sorry, Avatar). About few months later, I also watched The Hunger Games: Catching Fire in IMAX and that's when I truly got interested in IMAX cinematography and its aspect ratios, in which I found out that IMAX has aspect ratios of either 1.43:1 (generally for 15/70mm IMAX) or 1.90:1 (generally for digital IMAX) and I have been watching every single films that were shot in IMAX or have expanded IMAX aspect ratios for selected scenes since then.

On this page, I will be talking about films that were either shot with 15/70mm IMAX camera or digital IMAX camera. As of today, there are 58 of these films - The Dark Knight, Transformers: Revenge of the Fallen, Mission: Impossible - Ghost Protocol, The Dark Knight Rises, Star Trek Into Darkness, Riddick, The Hunger Games: Catching Fire, Transformers: Age of Extinction, Interstellar, Star Wars: Episode 7 - The Force Awakens, Batman v Superman: Dawn of Justice, Captain America: Civil War, Sully, Transformers: The Last Knight, Dunkirk, Avengers: Infinity War, First Man, and Avengers: Endgame.
